# GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) Playbook

> GEO is the academic/research-backed framework for optimizing content to be cited by generative AI. It's the cousin of AEO but with specific, validated techniques.

## Background

GEO was formalized in a 2023 paper from Princeton and Georgia Tech. They tested 10K+ queries across multiple LLMs and identified specific content changes that increased citation rates by up to 40%.

## The validated techniques (ranked by impact)

### 1. Cite sources (biggest impact)
Adding relevant citations to your content dramatically increases the chance an LLM cites it back. Don't just link — actually mention the source inline.

❌ "Studies show that 80% of users prefer video."
✅ "According to Wyzowl's 2024 State of Video Marketing report, 89% of marketers say video delivers a positive ROI."

### 2. Add statistics
Hard numbers are 2-3x more likely to be cited than vague claims. Always include specific numbers with sources.

### 3. Add quotations
Direct quotes from experts get cited. Interview, attribute, or quote authorities in your space.

### 4. Authoritative tone
Authoritative voice (active, declarative, expert) outperforms hedged or generic content. Write like you know what you're talking about.

### 5. Plain language
Avoid jargon where possible. LLMs parse plain English more reliably and surface it for general queries.

### 6. Easy-to-understand terms
If a 5th grader can understand it, an LLM can extract it. Break complex ideas into simple sentences.

### 7. Unique words
Avoid the same 200 words every other post uses. Specific vocabulary signals expertise and improves matching.

## How to apply GEO

For every blog post or article, after the first draft:

- [ ] Are 3+ specific statistics included? (with primary sources)
- [ ] Are 2+ expert quotes included? (or attributed observations)
- [ ] Is the tone authoritative without being preachy?
- [ ] Are jargon terms explained on first use?
- [ ] Are unique/precise words used (not "very good" but "exceptional")?
- [ ] Are external claims hyperlinked to primary sources?
- [ ] Is the structure scannable (H2s, bullets, tables)?

## GEO vs AEO vs SEO

| | SEO | AEO | GEO |
|---|-----|-----|-----|
| **Goal** | Rank in blue links | Be cited in AI answers | Maximize AI citation |
| **Optimize for** | Crawlers + ranking algo | Question-answer match | Content structure |
| **Key signal** | Backlinks, keywords | Direct answers, FAQ schema | Citations, stats, quotes |
| **Measurement** | SERP position | Citation frequency | Citation frequency + answer accuracy |

In practice: **all three stack.** A page that ranks well, answers questions directly, and follows GEO structure dominates across all surfaces.

## Tools for GEO

- **Manual:** search your questions in Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini, see who gets cited
- **Profound (paid):** tracks brand mentions across AI engines
- **Otterly (paid):** GEO monitoring
- **Free:** Spreadsheet with top 50 questions, monthly manual check

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Long intros that delay the value (LLMs time-out)
- ❌ Marketing copy without substantive content
- ❌ Claims without sources
- ❌ Content written for humans but with no extractable data points
- ❌ Copying competitor content (LLMs deprioritize near-duplicates)
